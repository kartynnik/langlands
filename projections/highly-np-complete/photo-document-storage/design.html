Shape dictionary as Nash equilibrium

The JB2 shape dictionary is a self-stabilizing set of locally-optimal glyph representations. No single glyph needs to be globally "correct" — each is the best strategy given the others. Cross-document shared dictionaries extend this to a global Nash equilibrium over a corpus. New glyph variants can coexist without forcing a full re-encoding.

Photo · Reconstruction Contract

Decoding runs the cascade in reverse: L0 reconstructs the global skeleton, each successive level blends in its residuals. The contradiction sidecar is applied last as a correction layer. Crucially, a missing sidecar does not corrupt the reconstruction — it degrades gracefully. The |⊤⟩ nodes are optional in the logical sense: their absence resolves to |⊥⟩, not to explosion.

Document · Cross-Page Consistency

The JB2 dictionary can be shared across pages of a document — this is the global Nash equilibrium layer operating above the per-page local one. A shape that appears on page 1 is reused on page 47 via a dictionary reference. But each page can also contain local variants without forcing a global re-encoding. The cascade of consistency operates at three levels: corpus → document → page → glyph.

The Fano–DjVu Duality

Both systems share the same structural move: instead of enforcing global consistency at encoding time (which is the explosion risk), they route contradictions to increasingly local contexts where they can be handled with an appropriately sized noise budget. The Fano cascade does this temporally across levels; DjVu does it spatially across layers. Same meta-library pattern, two different axes of instantiation.

Open Slot: p-adic Fault Detection

The Resonance Gate's p-adic phase e^{iφ_p} maps here as a cross-block modularity check: a block's Fano statistics are "modular" if they fit the parent level's model within σ_k. "Non-modular" blocks (Frey curve analogy) trigger quarantine. This gives an algebraic fault signature: blocks with non-modular signatures are structurally flagged before expensive residual coding begins.

Both designs are instantiations of the same meta-library.
